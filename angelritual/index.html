Steps of Skincare

A basic skincare routine typically involves several steps to help cleanse, moisturize, and protect the skin. Keep in mind that individual skincare needs may vary, and some steps can be adjusted based on personal preferences and specific skin concerns. Here's a general guide to a typical skincare routine:

1

Cleanser

Use a gentle cleanser to remove dirt, oil, and makeup from your skin. Cleansing helps prepare your skin for the subsequent skincare steps.
2

Toner

Apply a toner to help balance the skin's pH levels. Toners can also remove any remaining traces of dirt or oil and provide a base for the next products.
3

Serum

If you use any targeted treatments, such as serums or spot treatments, apply them at this stage. Common ingredients include vitamin C for brightening, hyaluronic acid for hydration, or retinoids for anti-aging.
4

Moisturizer

Apply a moisturizer to hydrate and lock in moisture. Choose a moisturizer suitable for your skin type, whether it's oily, dry, combination, or sensitive.
5

Sunscreen

In the morning, finish with a broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30. Sunscreen helps protect your skin from harmful UV rays and prevents premature aging
